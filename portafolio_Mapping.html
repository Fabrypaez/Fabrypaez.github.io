Video Mapping

Video mapping is the next exciting step in delivering bespoke visual content. It involves transforming an object's interior or exterior surfaces, such as a building, into a surface for video projection. Effective video mapping is a versatile and intriguing technology that can create incredible visuals full of colour and fluidity. Through the process, static objects can come alive.

Concept

Day of the Dead, or Día de Muertos, is one of the most important celebrations in Mexico, with roots dating back thousands of years, long before Spanish settlers arrived. It has become a blend of Catholic tradition and Mexican mysticism, commemorating death as another element of life and a way to remember and honour loved ones.

Project Development

The video mapping process involves many stages. It uses many graphics techniques like vector design, motion graphics, animation 2D and 3D animation, and sound effects, meaning that a video mapping production involves professionals with different backgrounds. I selected this technique because I have experience in this field and was inspired by the Animated Pop-up Book "The Ice book" made by Davy & Kristin McGuire several years. With my favourite season of the year nearby, "Day of Dead", I saw the opportunity to create my version of the Video Mapping Pop-up. The first stage was to think on the surface to project. In México, we have the tradition of making an Altar to honour people who have passed away. So I decided to create an Altar Pop-up and represent its items using animation.

Vector Design

The second part was the design of the items of the Altar in vectors. This process involves the conceptualization and abstraction of each item found on an Altar of the dead. For this part, I used Adobe Illustrator. I have to say that I am not good at drawing or doing vectors, so I used a pack of different vectors I had. I gave them the same aesthetic to unify all the vector designs. This vector design was also my base grid to do a test to build the Altar Pop-up and make some test of video projection.

2D Animation

Once I had the design, my next step was to analyze each aspect and select the ones I would animate in 2D and 3D. I used Adobe Illustrator to separate each vector into layers and After Effects to give them animation. The animation of these items is quite simple. It has animations such as rotations, positions, scales, opacity, colour and particles.

3D Animation

The next step was also a combination of different software. In this case, Cinema 4D to create the 3D model and animation 3D; Illustrator to export the textures that I would use in these models, for example, the animation of the paper. I used Cinema 4D in the candle, but I gave all these elements the post-production in After Effects.

Video Post-production

I decided to include one tiny part of a video for the protagonists of the Altar; at the beginning. I remembered the motion paintings and motion pictures used in Harry Potter movies. Then, I select two videos that symbolize the Catrines and Catrinas in Mexican culture. I gave them a remapping time using After Effects and reversed the videos because of their short duration.

Motion Graphics

We can find motion graphics around this project, but I want to write specifically about the ones I generated using only After Effects. I used particles and modified them using the Trapcode Particular plugin to create fire, smoke, rain, and space.

Digital Collage

As can be seen, I finished with a combination of different digital techniques: vectors, videos, motion graphics, animation 2D and 3D, which I called Digital Collage. Still, I was also meticulous in unifying the whole design. In this way, everything would look in the same style. Regarding the video, I decided not to change the aesthetic because the face painting that the actors used was enough to represent the concept.

Sound Design

I had never designed sound production; I always worked with a sound artist; that process is more straightforward than doing it. I gave him the animation and the idea of what I wanted, and he made his magic. I knew this part would be difficult, but it wasn't. I enjoyed it a lot. First, I had to analyze the animation and decide what music I would use and the sound effects. I wrote a technical script, which supported me in this process. The next step was to search for sound elements, but there are plenty of websites where you can access music and sound resources.

Video Mapping Process

Once I finished with the sound design, I started mounting the Altar Pop-up. I waited until the evening to have total control of the lights and set up the video projector. I used Isadora as the mapping software. The main difficulty was obtaining a good video projector. I realized that the colours changed a lot; if the video projector is low gamma, the video will have low quality.
