A bit of History.

The Bon Secours Mother and Baby Home are also known as St Mary's Mother and Baby Home. Located in Tuam, Galway, Ireland. It was where women and girls from across Mayo and Galway were sent to have babies out of wedlock between 1925 and 1961. The Irish Government knocked down the place in 1972 to make way for the Dublin Road housing estate a half-mile outside Tuam town. But, in the corner of a green left in the middle of the estate, some children uncovered a big tank with a collection of skulls and bones beneath a pile of rubble. According to the local historian Catherine Corless, the unmarked grave was the final resting place for 798 dead children; infants just days old, many toddlers and children as old as eight; most were buried only in shrouds without coffins.

Project Development.

For this project, I decided to do a digital video installation with the investigation data and the timeline of this terrible event. The main structure is an abstraction of the logo of the Bon Secours Sister. I searched a series of pictures related to each period this situation had. I started with the past, then the investigation's process, followed by the memorials, protests, and photos of families seeking their origins. Finally, the apologies from the Irish government and religious congregation authorities headed. Black and white are the colour base and old 3D television models that symbolize the past and how this news was scattered and taken into account by the Irish government due to international pressure.

3D Models and Video Post-production.

I chose 3D models of old TVs because of the era in which the facts happened, apart from the aesthetics of these televisions being more significant than the ones we currently find.

Each of the nine televisions has a different video that refers to the Tuam Mother and Baby Home events. I decided to respect the aesthetics of old televisions and the glitch errors of that time. For the digital image processing and to avoid author problems, the images were treated, so they were not presented as such. Glitch bugs, gameplay with layer placement, and white noise video on televisions accentuate this idea. The black and white colour of the photos refers to the past, mourning and catastrophe. A count marks the beginning of the era of terror in Ireland. We can see various events on other TV during that timeline. I wanted to add a tribute to the 798 children's deaths; by showing each of their names, ages, and years of their death on the top of this TV installation.

Digital Montage.

The final edition of the videos was assembled as follows:

It starts with a timeline of the events that occurred. In the second level, we find the Tuam Mother and Baby Home's past and the protests carried out over these years. In the third level, we see images of the investigation, the memorials made and the families that found their lost inheritance. On the upper level of the three televisions, we see the names of each of the children found in the Tuam Mother and Baby Home investigation.

Virtual Lighting.

For this virtual installation, the colour blue represents the topic discussed since it is a cold colour associated with death and spirituality. The lighting tests were to define and highlight the abstraction of the Bon Secours Sister logo in the shadows of this installation. After Effects, the software that I used to create this game of three-dimensionality and shadows, I knew that using this technique was risky due to the rendering time involved in the process. Still, I wanted to simulate how the exhibition could be seen in an art gallery.

Conclusion.

It is often difficult to talk about sensitive issues such as this case, even more so if they are represented with images and video. One in-depth analysis of the situation is needed to address the problem and empathise with the content. In my case, it was not difficult to handle this information because, unfortunately, in my country, this would be an issue that has been normalising in recent years, the deaths of the innocent in charge of an imposed system. I found in a surprising way how Mexico and Ireland have many similarities. It was not difficult for me to address this issue since I have previous experience researching and conceptualising social problems. However, I would have liked to set up an exhibition like Nam June Paik or Susan Hiller; This is the presentation format that I would give my audiovisual proposal.
