Our Share Space

Living with roommates may be challenging. Even if you are closest friends or romantic partners, negotiating expenses and making a flatmate chore list is not exactly fun. Thankfully, there is a flatmate app that can make almost any task more enjoyable and practical.

USER PERSONA

User personas are archetypical users whose goals and characteristics represent theneeds of a larger group of users. Here we can see a description including behaviour patterns, goals, skills, attitudes, background information, and the environment in which a persona operates. (Faller).

The research of this app is based on the analysis of millennials who work, study and have little time to organize the chores in the house.

Wireframes Design

When we work with low-fidelity design is understood as a representation of the final design that shows only the elements relevant to the analysis, regardless of the support. When it comes to a sketch, the low-fidelity design is called a WireFrame. (Marron Carmona). Using wireframes can help programmers and designers communicate the architecture of the application they are building. Wireframes do not represent the final design, but they can save design time and reduce adjustment efforts.

For this project, the following Wireframes were designed for the application "Our Share Space" to represent the layout of the content and where everything will reside on the user interface. Wireflow

Wireflows combine the benefits of wireframes and flow diagrams. The term wireflow was coined by Nielsen Norman Group after observing the practice emerging in the field. Wireflows visually show how parts of the interface change as the user interacts with the application. Additionally, annotations may be added to indicate such factors as the transmission of data within the system (Angeles).

The next Wireflow was designed for the early stage of prototyping the mobile application "Our Share Space" to represent all the functionalities and interactions that the product offers.

A prototype represents the final product, including simulations of the user interface interactions. Prototyping is the first phase in which designers can actually interact with their creations (Babich). DOCUMENTATION

UX Design

UX design is about shaping the experience of using a product, and most of that experience involves some interaction between the user and the product. UX design has a working process from abstract to concrete, and that working process can break down into five elements: strategy, scope, structure, skeleton, and surface. It involves user research (finding out who the users are in the first place), creating user personas (why and under what conditions would they use the product), performing user testing and usability testing, etc. (Siang)

Mobile UX Design.

Mobile UX design is the design of user experiences for hand-held and wearable devices. Designers create solutions (typically applications) to meet mobile users' unique requirements and restrictions. Designers focus on accessibility, discoverability and efficiency to optimise on-the-go interactive experiences (Siang).

I decided to create a mobile application for this assignment to achieve those user experiences before mentioned. Thus, this mobile application will require an internet connection; it won't need cloud services or links to other devices. However, it will need an external application to complete the payment option. The only technology that this product will need is a smartphone to function fully.

Following the five dimensions of interaction design: words, visual representations, physical objects or space, time and behaviour. (Hassenzahl). In this app, the user will be able to interact with Words such as button labels and blocks of text; Visual representations like images, typography and icons. The physical object will be a smartphone and its interaction with the user's fingers. The time dimension mainly refers to media that changes with time; this app will have small animations. Finally, in the dimension of behaviour, the user will have feedback when a process succeeds or fails. With the five dimensions established, we can see also the type of data that this app will use: numerical data types, whole numbers (int) and decimal numbers (double); a boolean condition with the payment method and, the characters data types and strings will be the mains data types in this app. (Hodge)

Developing Our Share Space App.

The app Our Share Space used the process of design thinking. The design thinking methodology refers to exploratory and iterative methods to better understand people and their problems, needs, and expectations. We must identify and challenge our assumptions about our products and the people who use them and suggest new strategies and solutions for better products and services (Hogue). Although there are multiple models for design thinking, I decided to use the Design Council Double Diamond:

UX Foundations: Prototyping - Cronenwett, Diane

Being a flatmate and sharing a house is difficult, especially when we come from different nationalities; our habits and behaviour are entirely different. I noticed that problem in my current home, and by using the methodology of the double diamond of Desing Thinking, I started my research. In the first phase, I focused on discovering as much information as possible; I began by observing all my flatmates and finding the main issues in the house's organisation that helped clarify the problem. In the second phase, I explored possible solutions, and then I remembered that we use an app to split expenses, and I got an idea what if that app could do more for us; if we could split chores, groceries and add events we could have a whole organisation of the house. And it was so that I could define the main functionalities of my product:

  • The organisation of the house's chores.
  • The organisation of the house's groceries.
  • Bill expenses split.
  • The organisation of the house's chores
  • House group chat.

Once I had all these aspects in mind, the following step was to start the prototype phase.

Design Patterns

Good navigation is a vehicle that takes users where they want to go. Creating good navigation is an essential part of mobile app design. Various navigation patterns have been devised to solve this challenge in different ways, but they all suffer from various usability problems (Babich). Following the guideline of Nick Babich in his article Essential Patterns of Mobile Navigation and the LinkedIn course Interaction Design: Software and Web Design Patterns with Diane Cronenwett, I could select and better understand what patterns I needed for my application.

Design Principles

While studying graphic design, I learnt about Gestalt psychology principles. I was not surprised that these principles were used when designing the user interface because the Gestalt principles help us understand how information is perceived. In this early stage of the low fidelity prototype, I mostly used two of these principles: proximity and similarity. We can observe in all the wireframes of Our Share Space App the application of these principles.

Application Comparison

When I started to create the Our Share Space App, I did a bit of digging into similar applications. I discovered a potential market for this kind of application. I found that little apps work in Ireland. I downloaded a few of these apps to study their functionality and interface. I selected two to guide me: Cozi and Splitwise. The former I had never used before, but the latter is the app that I use with my flatmates to organise the expenses and bills of the house. These applications are designed for the same user experience. They work only on a mobile platform. Their interface is inductive; one task per screen, easy screen controls, obvious next step, consistent screen template and progressive disclosure; however, the design of the Cozi app is a little difficult to understand. In Our Share Space App, I used a minimalist design that is easy to follow and understand. Next, I will explain the comparison of some functions of these applications with Our Share Space App.

Cozi App: The entire household can access a shared calendar that displays and coordinates everyone's schedules, to-do lists and the household grocery list. The recipe section makes it easy to plan meals and remember dishes you want to try. There is even a list library full of planning checklists for vacations, birthday parties and important events (Underlee).

The Best Chore Apps for Roommates

Splitwise App: You can categorise expenses, track balances, pay your roommates back for your share of a bill and add costs on the go if something unexpected happens. Pay by percentage, a certain share of the bill or a customised amount (Underlee).

The Best Chore Apps for Roommates

The first step in all these applications is an authorization process of creating an account; you may need to fill in information about yourself such as name, email and set a password, which you will be sharing with the rest of the house tenants.

Authorization process: create an account

One of the main features of the Cozi app is the organisation of events, where we can see a calendar interaction like in Our Share Space App. However, the main difference is how this data is displayed; in the former, we can see a list, and in the latter, we can see the design of a normal calendar. In both apps, we can add events through the plus symbol, leading us to the next part to fill in the event's information; these text inputs are very similar.

On the other hand, the main feature of the Splitwise app is to split expenses between the house tenants. Like in Our Share Space App, is it easy to set a bill and break it in equally; you can also add a photo of the receipt and make the payment through the app. The interface is easy to follow and understand.
