About.

Rituales is an interdisciplinary multimedia performance resulting from exploring generative art and interactive tools, emphasising their interaction with the body's movement and digital tech. This project focuses on the research and method that performative arts have been taking with the introduction of digital technologies and their relationship to different art disciplines.

This multimedia performance presents an abstract and personal inner journey when each element allows the body to express itself in a natural state in constant interaction with video projections and digital sensors. Through this, one two-way communication is carried out, the technology that affects the choreography and, in return, the movement that interacts with the video projection, finding points, moments and spaces of communication on the scene.

Rituales uses a design process based on the mise-en-scène when the design of each part of the performance is developed strategically. An interdisciplinary ceremony where the performance's custom interacts becomes one with the graphics, where the music impacts the body movements, where the lighting and the video projection do not fight each other, where the darkness allows us to see clearly, and all the stage elements converge harmoniously.

Rituales' digital scenography transforms its space but changes as the performer dances. In this performance, the sensory technologies enable us to experience and transcend human movement into a virtual space. In addition, the video mapping with its sound design allows us to create an immersive experience that surrounds us all. Everything was possible thanks to the creative team of Rituales: Tanya Covarrubias, writer and performer; Sandra Lanuza, musician and Fabry Páez, digital media artist.
