Technique

Video mapping projection is one of the fundamental approaches to perform augmented spatial reality (AR). This technique can provide AR experiences by taking videos, animations, vectors, images or any digital material and projecting them to provide the AR experience. Without the need in between of a mobile device or any digital device. Viewers can appreciate how objects come to life in a natural field of view and, in this way, create an immersive experience.

An interactive wall uses circuits to generate the interaction with the user, creating an input/output reaction between the computer and the user. In this case, electronic paint is used as the medium. This paint has small conductive carbon nanoparticles that allow data transmission.

Project Development

Among the elements that make up the multimedia production methodology is the multimedia production process, which is approached from three phases: pre-production, production, and post-production. In this order of ideas, each of the phases can be analysed, studied, and if necessary corrected, providing a flexible methodology, adaptable to changes and in which the creative process has a more significant role. Each phase will have a guide that provides a review and feedback discussed in conjunction with the development team and that the client can request at any time.

The project's pre-production will consist of developing a creative concept and analysing the space in which the interactive video mapping will be presented. With that analysis, all the project's measurements will be obtained, and the base structure will be designed, which will serve as the basis for the SAR and user interaction. This information will give us an idea of the technical equipment and the supplies we will need. In this stage, a narrative of the interactive video mapping's production is also developed, which consists of writing the experience that the user will live during the show. The realization of the multimedia script is also fundamental; this one will consist of a document similar to the storyboard.

The next stage is digital content production; it consists of creating the visual pieces of the video mapping. The production of these pieces involves the creation of 2D and 3D animation, motion graphics, video, illustrations, sound design and in general, any visual content that will be part of the final audiovisual product. We can include a sub-stage that consists of the assembly phase, where the electronic sensors would be involved in this stage.

The post-production phase is the last stage in creating interactive video mapping montages. It has to carry out the final tests in which the elements that may still be pending will evaluate to carry out the final product as planned from the pre-production phase. In this stage, not only digital content adjustments are made. It is the stage where the technical equipment is evaluated and suitable adjustments are made.

In the post-production stage, we find a variety of tests; these tests include interactivity, the projection of video mapping content linear, the production of sound effects, and the evolution of interactive dynamics.

New productions, technology, and experiences emerge through interactive video mapping, transforming new work teams into transdisciplinary teams despite the digitization era. The need to continue adapting new methodological models in which artists, engineers and others professionals will interact at various points to carry out the artistic ideas that our era is proposing is evident.
