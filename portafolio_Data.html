Introduction

The issue of femicide in Mexico has risen global visibility in the last couple of years. According to the National Institute of Statistics and Geography of Mexico, 3,952 women had died in 2020 due to femicides (INEGI). This data visualization intends to create awareness of the reality of this problem in a country where they are murdered just for being women.

With this impacting number in mind, I started to research the graphics that these data were presented. I found a lot of posters, videos, flyers, and infographics. Then considering all these options, I decided to put a significant challenge and create the data visualization using programmation. I wanted to show this number clearly and powerfully by using graphics and sound. One news that impacted me more was the article from the Excelsior, which showed the number of girls murdered. In this manner, I separated the femicide figures into three groups: girls from 0-14 years old, teenagers from 15-19 years old, and women 20-+85 years old. All this information is available in the National Institute of Statistics and Geography of Mexico (INEGI).

Project Development

The colour purple is a constant in all the campaigns against femicides in Mexico. I divided each group by using different shades of purple in circles; each circle would represent one lost life. One of the meanings that we find in the symbolism of the circle is unity (Bonet), which is the message of all feminist marches in Mexico. In choosing the sound, I decided to use laughter since it has a powerful emotional charge; all these victims should be alive, enjoying life and laughing at it. With all these elements, I want to emphasize the severe and heartbreaking situation suffered by women in Mexico.
